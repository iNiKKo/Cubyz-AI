## Summary
This document outlines game design principles for Cubyz, focusing on progression, break the cycle, player engagement, and balancing.

## Explanation
Cubyz is a voxel sandbox game with an infinite world, focused on great adventures and immersive exploration; players are given freedom in abundance to choose their own path. Progression encourages interacting with all mechanics (explore to build, survive to explore, strategize to survive, build to strategize). "Break the Cycle" design rules, each with its specific reason: No Dimensions -- separate places are instead fit physically into Cubyz's massive world for the player to come across, rather than being split into separate dimensions. No Teleportation -- it makes the game less immersive, diminishing the exploring aspect and the player's sense of the world's scale. No Automation -- quick, infinite resources at the player's fingertips would discourage exploration, since the player would never need to forage or search for blocks. Mobs Don't Respawn Naturally -- clearing a dangerous area makes it safe to build/explore, and this also prevents mob farming. No Passive Animals -- animals don't want to die, so they flee or defend themselves, forcing the player to strategize to hunt. No Unbreakable Tools -- getting too attached to one unbreakable tool would mean players never make other tool types, and low-tier materials would see less use. Avoid Clutter -- find uses for existing items before adding new ones, and prevent unwanted items from entering the player's inventory. Use Realism Tastefully -- Cubyz is whimsical, not realistic; real-world inspiration is fine but nothing is copied without a good functional reason. Avoid Excessive Additions -- a variant/addition needs a genuine functional reason; try modifying existing content before adding new content. Function over Form -- a mechanic's function to gameplay must be considered before its design/theme is finalized, or it risks feeling pointless.
