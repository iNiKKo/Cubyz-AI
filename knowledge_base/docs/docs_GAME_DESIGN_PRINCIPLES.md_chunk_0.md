## Explanation
**Source context: this is Cubyz's internal Game Design Principles document, written for contributors, describing design goals and intended direction -- not a feature list of what's currently in the shipped game.** If asked whether a specific mechanic described here (e.g. enemies, invasions, bosses) currently exists in Cubyz, say that it's a stated design goal/direction rather than confirming it's implemented, unless another source explicitly confirms it's shipped.

Cubyz is a voxel sandbox game with an infinite world and a focus on great adventures, immersive exploration with numerous challenges along the way. Freedom is cherished and given to players in abundance, so they can choose their own path through the world.

Progression encourages interacting with different mechanics, such as exploring, building, crafting, etc. Examples: Explore to Build (the player needs to interact with exploration to get the resources they want for building); Survive to Explore (caves and structures may have enemies and traps that the player has to face if they want to explore in peace); Strategize to Survive (prepare for a trip with tools, weapons, and gear in order to make survival easier); Build to Strategize (the player has to build defenses for events, invasions, and bosses, and also needs to upgrade their workstations to allow for better gear).
