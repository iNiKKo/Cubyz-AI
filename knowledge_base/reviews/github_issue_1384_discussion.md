## Explanation
The Tree Tapping feature introduces a new mechanic that allows players to extract sap and resin from certain logs, providing a renewable resource system for obtaining materials like sap, resin, syrup, and amber. This mechanic includes crafting tappers, placing them on sappy logs, collecting resin in buckets, and processing it into usable items.

Sappy logs are unique variants of regular logs that drop 1 log and 1 sap/resin when broken. Players can place tappers on sappy logs to start tapping them; only one tapper per log is allowed. Tapping involves right-clicking the tapper with a bucket (same as #627) to collect resin, which fills up over time until the bucket is full. When the bucket is filled, players can collect a 'raw resin bucket' and cook it in a furnace to get the resin item used for tools or crafting.

The mechanic also includes durability for tappers, which should be preserved after placing them on logs. Sappy logs can deplete over time, turning into regular logs after tapping multiple times. Players can right-click a log with resin to turn it into a decorative resinous log that does not drop particles and cannot be tapped further.

When the bucket is filled with resin, players can collect the 'raw resin bucket' and cook it in a furnace to get the resin item used for tools or crafting. The water evaporates during cooking, and the player keeps the bucket.
