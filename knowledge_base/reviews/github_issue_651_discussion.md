## Explanation
The discussion revolves around adding new combat effects to make gameplay more strategic and interesting. The maintainer comments suggest refining existing ideas like 'bleeding' to ensure it stacks damage over time without immediate removal, while also proposing new effects such as fatigue (reducing energy refill effectiveness instead of preventing eating), pheromones (making specific mobs ignore the player), blizzard (increasing movement speed and extra damage after killing enemies), blood lust (increasing regeneration and life steal after kills), mirror (creating temporary mirror images on being hit), synergy (strengthening existing effects), death and destruction (causing a fiery explosion when health drops below 10%), curse of explosions (making killed enemies explode and spread the curse), ruler of <x> (extra damage in specific biomes), shield (providing non-refillable extra HP), giants decotion (increasing bag capacity temporarily with item drop on effect end), elementalist (dealing true damage by mixing multiple types), adaptation (gaining resistance for each received damage type), specialist of X (gaining bonuses for killing specific mob types), generalist (gaining bonuses for killing unique mob types), cleanse (removing effects and restoring health/energy with a risk to the opponent), electric spark (causing electric arcs between enemies on attack), child of moon/child of sun (extra damage and resistance during night/day), coward (burst speed/jump boost away from entities, stun/blind if surrounded), last chance (becoming immortal for a short time after lethal damage with healing/cleansing on killing an enemy), giant slayer (extra damage towards enemies with more health), king slayer (extra damage against enemies wearing precious materials), protector of the weak (extra damage if friendly non-aggressive mobs are nearby), last stand (extra damage when fighting multiple enemies), and juggernaut (running into enemies deals damage and knocks them back). The maintainer also notes that some effects might be similar to those found in games like Witcher 3 and League of Legends, aiming for a balanced and engaging experience.
