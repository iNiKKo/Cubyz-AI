## Explanation
The issue discusses various climbing mechanics in Cubyz, including free and lock-on climbing styles, ledge grabbing, and potential control schemes. The essentials include keeping the player stationary while climbing and allowing outside forces to affect them. Free climbing involves automatic or manual start options: you automatically start as soon as you're off the ground or have to hold `jump` or `sneak`. In free climbing, you can freely move side-to-side, back and forth, and up and down. If climbing is automatic, you can only let go by moving out of reach of any climbable blocks; if manual, a control for letting go could be added. Lock-on climbing requires interaction with a climbable block to start climbing, allowing limited movement on one or two axes. Interaction with another climbable block lets you 'jump' to it within a limited reach distance. Limited interactions like attacking, placing, or breaking blocks are possible while stationary. Ledge grabbing is proposed for vertical exploration, where the speed of climbing decreases as the distance from the ledge increases. The discussion also touches on accessibility concerns, such as the potential nightmare of manual climbing for people with RSI. Potential gating mechanisms include food levels and health, which could affect grab speeds. Alternative solutions like elevators that provide anti-gravity or wind are suggested to mitigate climbing issues. There is a proposal for tiered climbing speeds using different materials or equipment, similar to tools. The maintainer proposes keeping the control scheme simple, possibly using mouse buttons to alternate between left and right at a specific interval to climb faster based on accuracy. Another suggestion is to use boots with tiered climbing speeds, where different amounts of materials can be used to achieve various speeds.
