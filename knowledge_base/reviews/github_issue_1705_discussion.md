## Explanation
The discussion revolves around establishing a policy for content suggestions in Cubyz, emphasizing that suggestions should be demonstrated through addons or mods and align with the game's design principles. Maintainers propose refining these principles to ensure consistency. The proposed policy requires that content suggestions be demonstrated through addons or mods, come from active contributors, and align with the game's design principles. These principles include ensuring new blocks are unique (filling underrepresented niches) and versatile (usable across multiple biomes), and considering whether players care about the new block. Maintainers suggest refining these principles to have clear guidelines for rejecting suggestions, particularly regarding what constitutes clutter. The issue template will be updated with a detailed explanation of the content suggestion process, including specific design principles. The link to this detailed explanation is [https://github.com/PixelGuys/Cubyz/blob/master/docs/CONTENT_SUGGESTIONS.md](https://github.com/PixelGuys/Cubyz/blob/master/docs/CONTENT_SUGGESTIONS.md). Specifically, the game design principles include that decoration blocks count as clutter and have a yes/no answer explicitly included in these principles.
