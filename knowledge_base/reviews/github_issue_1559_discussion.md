## Explanation
Discussion about implementing a critical damage mechanic for tools when they reach their last durability point.

The issue proposes adding a 'Breath of the Wild'-style mechanic where tools deal critical damage upon breaking. Specifically, on the last point of durability, your tool performs critical damage before breaking, allowing you to break the block you were mining without needing to switch tools. This encourages players to use their tools until they break and makes the `single_use` modifier more useful.

The maintainer asked how big the critical damage should be, but no specific value was provided in the discussion. One user suggested that a 2x multiplier against players could lead to a PvP meta with players running around with almost-broken weapons. Another user proposed that the crit multiplier should differ between players and monsters, suggesting different values for each.

The discussion also touched on potential game balance implications, such as creating a meta where players intentionally use almost-broken weapons for an advantage in player-versus-player (PvP) scenarios.
