## Summary
Discussion on preventing players from mining through dungeons by implementing various mechanisms such as special blocks, fatigue effects, or unique crafting materials.

## Explanation
The discussion revolves around preventing players from mining through dungeons while maintaining engaging gameplay. The maintainers explore various mechanisms to achieve this balance:

1. **Special Crafting Materials**: Each dungeon type would require a unique crafting material, ensuring that players must obtain specific items to progress.
2. **Fatigue Effect**: Similar to Minecraft's ocean temples, a fatigue effect could be implemented where mining is restricted until the main boss of the dungeon is defeated.
3. **Protector Block**: A protector block similar to Rust game cupboards can be used to restrict mining within an area. Players would need direct access to this block to modify the environment.
4. **Block Defense Increase**: Blocks could have their defense increased in certain areas, making them harder but still mineable with effort.
5. **Blocks Regeneration**: Blocks could regenerate quickly after being mined, forcing players to find alternative methods of progression.
6. **Alternative Solutions**: Instead of restricting mining entirely, the maintainers suggest providing tools and items that encourage creative problem-solving. For example:
   - Building wings from cloth and wood for flying over difficult parkour sections.
   - Improving armor and weapons to defeat strong enemies without direct combat.
   - Using throwable bombs to destroy obstacles or enemies from a distance.

The goal is to create challenging puzzles and traps that require players to think creatively rather than relying on brute force mining.
